Citing LinkedIn:
Thomas (Tom) Pestak has 10 years’ experience as an engineer, manager, and project leader in the management, planning and execution of Research, Development, Test and Evaluation (RDT&E) projects involving complex hardware/software systems for the Department of Defense (DoD). His career progression involves increasing levels of technical complexity and management responsibility. He is currently the Director of Software Development at The Perduco Group, where he manages all aspects the company’s growing portfolio of software projects for DoD and commercial customers. Previously, during a 16-month assignment with the Air Force Research Laboratory’s (AFRL) Center for Rapid Innovation, he architected and led a number of rapid reaction technology, business, and infrastructure initiatives, including an indoor gunshot detection system. Prior to joining The Perduco Group, Mr. Pestak was a research engineer in the Autonomy & Navigation Technology (ANT) Center at the Air Force Institute of Technology (AFIT) where he advanced the technology readiness levels of Positioning, Navigation, and Timing (PNT) technologies for the DoD. He led numerous AFIT research projects and collaborated with the AFRL and the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA). In addition to his extensive experience as a project manager, researcher, and engineer, Mr. Pestak has excellent communication, technical writing, and leadership skills. He has supported business development efforts and has written several proposals which resulted in new projects being awarded. Mr. Pestak is currently an adjunct professor in the college of engineering and computer science at Wright State University, teaching EGR3350: Technical Communications for Engineers and Computer Scientists. He owns and contributes to www.cavstheblog.com and enjoys spending time with his family.
